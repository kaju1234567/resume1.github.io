Buddhist
Religious Festivals

There are several Buddhist festivals that provide time for Buddhist to reflect and celebrate their faith.
The two most commonly celebrated festivals are Wesak and Parinirvana Day.

Wesak

Wesak, also spelled Vesak, Sanskrit Vaishakha, Pali Vesakha, most important of the Theravada Buddhist festivals, commemorating the birth, enlightenment, and death of the Buddha. The event is observed on the full-moon day of the lunar month Vesakha, which falls in April or May

Parinirvana

Parinirvana Day is an annual festival, observed in some Mahayana countries, that remembers the death of the Buddha when he reached the age of 80. When the Buddha died, Buddhists believe that he entered a state called Parinirvana, which means Nirvana without end.
